Terms

Terms of engagement.

Last updated 15 May 2026.

The office

Aldridge & Charles Marine ("the office") is a private marine office providing yacht management, brokerage, refit oversight, charter, crew placement, detailing, engineering counsel, and concierge services to a retained roster of principals.

Engagement

Every engagement begins with a written letter of appointment that sets the scope, the retainer, the term, the deliverables, and any pass-through costs. Nothing is agreed by handshake alone. Nothing is started without the letter.

Fees

The office charges by retainer for management, project, and crew engagements, and by commission for brokerage transactions. Retainers are paid quarterly in advance. Brokerage commission is paid at closing. Pass-through costs are billed at cost with all receipts shared.

No referral fees

The office accepts no commission from yards, suppliers, brokers, vendors, classification societies, or any third party recommended in the course of an engagement. Every recommendation is given on merit. Every invoice is audited.

Confidentiality

Every engagement is conducted under standing confidentiality. The office does not name its principals, its vessels, or its captains in any marketing, press, or website. Principals control all references made to their identity or their boat.

Termination

Retainer engagements may be terminated by either side on ninety days written notice. Brokerage mandates terminate on closing, on expiry, or on written withdrawal. Outstanding invoices and audited pass-through costs are settled at termination.

Governing law

Engagements are governed by English law, save where the principal’s jurisdiction is specified in the letter of appointment. Disputes are resolved by negotiation between principal and director first; if unresolved, by mediation in London or in the principal’s jurisdiction.

Site terms

This site is operated by Aldridge & Charles Marine. The content is provided for information. Nothing on the site constitutes a marine survey, a brokerage offer, a tax position, or legal advice. Engagement with the office is by written appointment, not by site visit.
